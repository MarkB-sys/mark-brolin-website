About Mark Brolin

Background

Mark Brolin is a London-based geostrategist and author. He holds a degree from the Stockholm School of Economics and has worked in the Swedish Foreign Ministry, Nasdaq/OMX, and Cantor Fitzgerald.

His transition to geopolitics and geoeconomics was shaped by the events of September 11, when he lost 658 colleagues. He has since worked with business leaders, policy specialists, research institutes, and think tanks across three continents.

Approach

RO

Balanced Assessment

Acknowledging risks alongside opportunities enables more effective decision-making. This balanced perspective helps leaders avoid both excessive caution and overconfidence.

AN

Signal vs Noise

Separating structural shifts from episodic events. Analysis focuses on what changes decisions: constraints, timelines, and implementation realities rather than surface-level commentary.

BU

Ground-Level Insights

Combining top-down policy analysis with bottom-up insights from implementation. This approach captures how policy meets practice and where change actually occurs.

PN

Independence

Politically independent and evidence-led, working across sectors and perspectives with a focus on incentives, trade-offs and practical outcomes.

How Mark Helps Leaders

Mark works with boards and senior leadership teams to translate geopolitical complexity into decision-ready insights. His briefings help leaders separate signal from noise, stress-test assumptions, and improve judgment under uncertainty.

The focus is on practical implications: how structural shifts affect strategy, risk, investment, security, regulation and capital allocation. Analysis examines constraints, timelines, and implementation realities rather than surface-level events.

This approach helps leaders identify early signals, reduce blind spots, and make more proportionate risk decisions. The goal is clearer strategic priorities and better timing across volatile geopolitical environments.

Media & commentary: Regular contributor to international media outlets.
